Your robots.txt might be quietly telling ChatGPT not to read your site.

aivisible-fix audits a website against 17 known AI crawlers — GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot, Google-Extended, and more — then writes the fix: a patched robots.txt and a starter llms.txt, ready to deploy. Not another score.

# what it checks

Six checks, weighted toward what actually causes invisibility.

40 pts

No AI crawler blocked

Checked per-agent against 17 known crawlers, not just a blanket Disallow.

20 pts

llms.txt present

The emerging llmstxt.org convention: a short, LLM-readable summary of your site.

15 pts

Sitemap valid

How crawlers discover your full page list instead of guessing from links.

15 pts

Structured data

JSON-LD is what answer engines lean on to extract facts without guessing.

5 pts

Sitemap in robots.txt

Exists-but-undiscoverable is nearly as bad as missing.

5 pts

robots.txt reachable

The first file every crawler reads.

# install & use

Two commands.

$ pip install aivisible-fix

# score any site
$ aivisible-fix audit https://example.com

# generate the fix — writes local files, publishes nothing
$ aivisible-fix fix https://example.com --name "Example Co" --description "..."
Wrote robots.fixed.txt
Wrote llms.txt
